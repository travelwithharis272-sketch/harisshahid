New Zealand Adds 47 Jobs to Work Visa List 2026: Full Details for Migrants 🇳🇿

By Haris Shahid · Updated June 20, 2026

New Zealand has made a major immigration update: from 9 March 2026, 47 new occupations were added to the National Occupation List (NOL) used for the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV). At the same time, the immigration median wage rose to NZD $35.00 per hour. If you're hoping to work in New Zealand, these changes open new doors — this guide explains exactly what changed and how to qualify.

Quick Summary
  • What: 47 new occupations added (skill levels 1–3)
  • When: Effective 9 March 2026
  • Visa: Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV)
  • New median wage: NZD $35.00/hour

What exactly changed?

Immigration New Zealand recognised 47 additional occupations at skill levels 1 to 3 under the NOL — the country's new job classification system that is gradually replacing the older ANZSCO system. Employers can submit Job Check applications for these newly added roles from 9 March 2026. A notable highlight is that several chef roles were reclassified into clearer categories based on seniority and skill.

The new median wage explained

From 9 March 2026, New Zealand's immigration median wage increased to NZD $35.00 per hour (up from $33.56). While the general median wage requirement for the AEWV was removed back in 2025, this figure still matters because it's used for:

  • Income thresholds for supporting a partner and dependent children
  • Advertising exemptions (roles paid at least twice the median wage)
  • Certain Green List and residence pathway settings

Who can apply for the AEWV?

The Accredited Employer Work Visa requires:

  1. A job offer from a New Zealand Accredited Employer (an employer approved by Immigration NZ to hire overseas workers)
  2. An eligible occupation — now including the 47 newly added roles
  3. Employer compliance — they must advertise the role for at least 14 days and try to hire New Zealanders first
  4. Meeting role requirements — some roles need registration, qualifications, or licences

Which sectors are in demand?

New Zealand's key in-demand sectors include healthcare, engineering, IT, education, construction, skilled trades, and primary industries. Job demand varies by region — cities tend to offer more IT and engineering roles, while rural areas focus on healthcare, teaching, and agriculture.

Pathway to residence

For skilled workers, New Zealand offers Green List pathways (Tier 1 Straight to Residence and Tier 2 Work to Residence). Applicants generally must be 55 or younger, hold a full-time job offer from an Accredited Employer, and meet role-specific wage and qualification requirements. This makes New Zealand attractive for those wanting a long-term future, not just temporary work.

How to prepare

  1. Check if your occupation is on the NOL or Green List
  2. Build a strong, NZ-style CV
  3. Target Accredited Employers when job hunting
  4. Get qualifications or registration recognised where required
  5. Ensure your English meets the visa requirement
