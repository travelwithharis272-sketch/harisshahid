What is the Portugal Job Seeker Visa?

The Portugal Job Seeker Visa is a national (long-stay) visa that allows citizens from outside the EU, EEA, and Switzerland to enter Portugal and look for employment without a prior job offer. It was introduced in October 2022 to help fill labour shortages in the country.

Unlike a work visa, you do not need an employer to sponsor you before you travel. You enter Portugal, attend interviews and job fairs, and once you sign a work contract, you apply for a residence permit to stay and work legally. In 2025, around 84% of job seeker visa holders found employment within their visa validity period.

Who is eligible for the Portugal Job Seeker Visa in 2026?

To qualify for the Portugal Job Seeker Visa in 2026, you must be a non-EU/EEA/Swiss citizen who can prove qualifications, financial means, and intent to work. The visa is now primarily aimed at skilled and highly qualified professionals.

You are eligible if you meet the following conditions:

  • You are a citizen of a country outside the EU, EEA, or Switzerland (Pakistan qualifies).
  • You hold a recognised higher education degree (Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD) OR have relevant professional experience (often 5+ years) in your field.
  • You can show financial means of around €3,280 (three times the Portuguese minimum wage).
  • You have a clean criminal record (issued within the last 90 days).
  • You are willing to register with IEFP, Portugal's public employment service.

From January 2026, Portugal began prioritising applicants with skills in sectors facing labour shortages, such as technology, healthcare, and engineering.

Documents required for the Portugal Job Seeker Visa

You need a complete set of documents to apply. Missing or uncertified paperwork is the most common reason for delays or rejection. Prepare the following:

  • Valid passport — valid for at least 3 months beyond your intended stay, with two blank pages.
  • Completed national visa application form, signed.
  • Two recent passport-size photos (colour, biometric standard).
  • Proof of financial means — certified bank statements from the last 3 months showing ~€3,280.
  • Proof of accommodation in Portugal (hotel booking, rental, or invitation).
  • Travel medical insurance — minimum €30,000 coverage.
  • Criminal record certificate — issued within the last 90 days, apostilled.
  • Round-trip flight reservation or proof of onward travel.
  • Motivation letter — a statement explaining your job-search plan.
  • Educational and professional certificates — degrees and experience letters.

Documents not in Portuguese or English usually need certified translation and apostille certification.

How much money do you need? (Financial requirement)

You must prove savings of around €3,280, which equals three times Portugal's minimum monthly wage for 2026. This shows you can support yourself for the first few months while job hunting.

Practical tips for the financial requirement:

  • Keep the funds in your account for at least 3 months before applying (a sudden large deposit looks suspicious).
  • Provide a certified bank statement, not a screenshot.
  • If you have a sponsor (a legal resident in Portugal), you can submit a sponsorship letter with proof of their income instead.

How to apply: Step-by-step process

The application follows a clear sequence. Apply early — processing typically takes 30 to 90 days, and applying between January and March is recommended for faster service.

  1. Gather all documents — passport, financial proof, insurance, criminal record, motivation letter, and qualifications.
  2. Book an appointment at the nearest Portuguese consulate or VFS Global centre in your country.
  3. Submit your application and pay the visa fee in person.
  4. Wait for the decision — usually 30 to 90 days.
  5. Travel to Portugal once approved (single entry within the 120-day validity).
  6. Register your address within 3 days of arrival and register with IEFP.
  7. Search for a job — attend interviews, job fairs, and networking events.
  8. Apply for a residence permit once you sign a work contract.

How to apply from Pakistan

Pakistani citizens can apply for the Portugal Job Seeker Visa through the Portuguese consulate or the authorised VFS Global centre in Pakistan. Pakistan is one of the top source countries for this visa, making up roughly 9% of applicants.

From Pakistan, pay special attention to: getting your degree and experience documents attested and apostilled, arranging a certified bank statement showing the required funds, and writing a clear motivation letter. Applying early in the year improves your processing time.

Advantages and limitations

Advantages

  • No prior job offer or employer sponsorship required.
  • Pathway to a residence permit and, eventually, citizenship.
  • Access to Portugal's job market, low cost of living, and EU lifestyle.
  • High success rate — 84% of holders found jobs within validity in 2025.

Limitations

  • Single entry only — if the visa expires before you find a job, you must leave.
  • You can only re-apply one year after the previous visa expired.
  • Now focused on skilled/qualified professionals, so qualifications matter.
  • Knowing basic Portuguese helps a lot when job hunting.
