New Zealand Work Visa (AEWV) 2026: How Pakistanis Can Apply

New Zealand's main work visa is the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV) — an employer-led visa that can run up to 5 years and, for many people, becomes a real pathway to permanent residence. Here's the honest 2026 guide for Pakistanis: what's required, the new wage rules, and the step-by-step process.

What is the AEWV?

The AEWV replaced the older Essential Skills visa in July 2022 and is now New Zealand's primary route for foreign workers. The key feature: it is employer-led. You can't just apply on your own — you need a full-time job offer (at least 30 hours a week) from an employer who is accredited under the scheme and has passed a Job Check for your role.

The big 2026 changes you should know

  • Median wage removed for general AEWV (since March 2025): employers now pay the market rate for the role plus at least minimum wage — not a fixed median wage.
  • New median wage from 9 March 2026: NZD $35.00/hour. This still matters for Green List pay floors, partner support, and skilled residence — just not general AEWV pay.
  • 47 new occupations added (March 2026) at skill levels 1–3 under the new National Occupation List (NOL).
  • Work experience requirement cut from 3 years to 2 years.
In plain terms: getting a New Zealand work visa in 2026 is more about landing a genuine job with an accredited employer than hitting a fixed salary number — though your pay must still match what a Kiwi would earn for the same role.

Requirements for Pakistanis

  • Job offer from an accredited NZ employer with an approved Job Check.
  • Skills threshold: either 2+ years of relevant work experience, or a recognised Level 4+ qualification.
  • Pay at market rate for the role (and at least NZ minimum wage).
  • English: required only for lower-skilled roles (skill level 4–5). Skill levels 1–3 don't need an English test.
  • Health & character: medical exam and police certificates.
  • Valid passport and genuine intent.

How to apply — the 3-step process

1

Employer accreditation

Your employer must be accredited under the AEWV scheme. You can check New Zealand's official accredited-employer list before applying — never rely on an unaccredited "agent."

2

Job Check

The employer applies for a Job Check for your specific role. For lower-skilled roles they usually must advertise locally (minimum 21 days) and show they tried to hire New Zealanders first. Once approved, you receive a job token.

3

Your AEWV application

With the job token, you submit your own visa application — proving your experience/qualification, English (if required), health, and character. If approved, you get your AEWV and can travel to New Zealand to start work.

Does it lead to residence?

The AEWV itself is temporary, but most people use it as a springboard to permanent residence. The main routes are:

  • Green List — Straight to Residence: if your job is on New Zealand's Green List, you may apply for residence immediately.
  • Green List — Work to Residence: for other listed roles, after 24 months of qualifying work.
  • Skilled Migrant Category (SMC): a points-based residence route once you have NZ work experience. See our comparison of skilled-migration routes for context.
Tip: If your occupation is on the Green List or among the 47 newly added roles, your path is much stronger. Check the official list before committing to a job offer.
Honest note: New Zealand is a genuine, well-run route — but it depends entirely on a real job with an accredited employer. Be cautious of anyone selling "guaranteed" NZ work visas or charging large fees up front. Always verify the employer on Immigration New Zealand's accredited list.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a job offer for the New Zealand AEWV?

Yes. The AEWV is employer-led. You must have a full-time job offer (at least 30 hours a week) from an accredited New Zealand employer who has an approved Job Check before you can apply.

Is the median wage still required for the AEWV?

No. The median wage requirement was removed from the general AEWV in March 2025. Employers must now pay the market rate for the role and at least the NZ minimum wage. The median wage (NZD $35/hour from 9 March 2026) still applies to Green List pay floors, partner support, and skilled residence.

Do I need IELTS for a New Zealand work visa?

English is only tested for lower-skilled roles (ANZSCO skill level 4 or 5). Skill levels 1 to 3 do not require an English test. You can also meet the requirement through citizenship of certain English-speaking countries or English-medium study.

Can the AEWV lead to residence in New Zealand?

Yes. The AEWV is temporary but is a springboard to residence through the Green List (Straight to Residence or Work to Residence after 24 months) and the points-based Skilled Migrant Category.
